# Prompt for designing a logo

Act as an expert Scandinavian graphic designer specializing in minimalist and modern aesthetics. Your task is to design a simple, stylish, and circular logo for the "NoFish" web application. This application is a marine weather forecasting tool for fishers, providing safety and fishing condition data along the Norwegian coast.

# Nano Banana prompt — NoFish logo (512 × 512)

Generate a square 512 × 512 pixel app logo for **NoFish**, a maritime fishing-forecast web app for small boats in Norway. Tagline: *"Because fishing in bad weather is worse than no fishing at all."* The mark is a safety tool — friendly but serious, never playful or childish.

## Concept

A bold, minimalist mark combining a stylized fish silhouette with a wave, droplet, or compass needle — evoking the sea, safety, and weather awareness. Strong silhouette first, decorative details second.

## Design requirements

1. **Style** — Scandinavian design principles: minimalist, clean, functional. Use **rounded, easy curves** to feel friendly and approachable. Flat vector look, geometric shapes, confident strokes. No 3D, no photorealism, no bevels, no drop shadows, no outer glow, no skeuomorphism.
2. **Shape** — The visible mark must be **circular**, symbolizing unity and inclusiveness. Compose it inside an inscribed circle of ~460 px diameter on the 512 × 512 canvas, leaving ~26 px of safe margin on every side. Nothing important may sit in the corners — the asset is CSS-masked into a circle at render time.
3. **Inspiration** — Take cues from the **yr.no** logo: a single, instantly readable graphic idea executed with maximum simplicity and clarity. No fine details, no small text, no gradients finer than a few broad steps. The mark must remain legible at **32 × 32 pixels**.
4. **Colors** — Use only NoFish brand colors:
   - **Maritime Teal** (primary): `#00796b`, `#00695c`, `#004d40`
   - **Coastal Red** (accent, used sparingly for one small highlight only): `#e53935`, `#d32f2f`, `#c62828`
   - Optional neutral: off-white `#f8fafc`
5. **Typography** — Prefer no text. If a letterform is essential, use a modern geometric sans-serif (Inter, Geist, or similar) at a weight that remains readable when the whole mark is scaled to 32 px.
6. **Versatility** — The result must work as an app icon, website header logo, favicon, and in print. That means: solid silhouette, high contrast against both light and dark surfaces, no element thinner than ~6 px at 512 × 512.

## Output

- **Canvas**: exactly 512 × 512 pixels, square.
- **Background**: fully transparent (alpha = 0). No background fill, no border, no plate behind the mark.
- **Format**: PNG, sRGB, transparent background.

## Do not include

Text labels, watermarks, photographic textures, drop shadows, outer glows, banded gradients, or any reference to other brands. The mark must be entirely original and not derivative of existing logos or icons. Avoid any elements that could be confused with common symbols (e.g., a simple fish silhouette that could be mistaken for a generic "fish" icon without the safety/weather context).
